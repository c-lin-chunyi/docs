# Appendix A: A Walkthrough of Common ANOVA Designs

This appendix walks through several named ANOVA designs that often appear in exams:

1. one-way completely randomized design;
2. randomized block design;
3. Latin square design;
4. factorial design;
5. one-factor repeated-measures ANOVA;
6. two-factor repeated-measures ANOVA;
7. mixed design.

For each design, we follow the same route:

$$
\begin{multline}
\text{design layout}
\rightarrow
\text{one-line linear model}
\rightarrow
\text{fitted means/components}
\rightarrow \\
\text{sum-of-squares decomposition}
\rightarrow
\text{ANOVA table}
\rightarrow
\text{common exam traps}.
\end{multline}
$$

 The main chapter has already developed the logic of least-squares fitting, residual error, and model comparison, and here we translate that logic into the bracket-form and ANOVA-table language commonly used in textbooks and exams.
